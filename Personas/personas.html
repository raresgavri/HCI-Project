Type: Primary (Persona with Myopia)

Name: Emily Thompson

Background
  • Date of Birth: 05/15/1990
  • Gender: Female
  • Location: New York City, USA
  • Work place: Organization Name, Marketing agency
  • School: Graduated with a Bachelor's degree in Marketing from a local university (if required)
  • Technology Level:Moderate; familiar with smartphones and basic apps
Main Points
  • Emily is a 33-year-old professional working in the marketing field in a busy urban environment.
  • She was diagnosed with myopia (nearsightedness) during her teenage years and has been wearing glasses or contact lenses ever since.
  • Emily relies heavily on her vision for her work, as she often needs to read and analyze documents, create visual presentations, and review marketing materials.
  • She uses a smartphone and computer extensively throughout the day for communication, research, and creative work.
Goals
  • Improve her productivity and efficiency at work by reducing eye strain and visual discomfort.
  • Enhance her ability to read and edit text and documents without straining her eyes.
  • Have access to tools that help her magnify small text or images for better visibility and comprehension.
  • Edit and enhance images to create visually appealing marketing materials.
Frustrations and Pain Points
  • Constant eye strain and fatigue due to prolonged use of screens and reading small text.
  • Difficulty reading and editing documents with small font sizes, leading to errors and reduced productivity.
  • Inability to view and appreciate images or graphics that are too small or lack clarity.
  • The need to rely on others for assistance with picture editing, leading to delays and a lack of creative control.
Detailed Description

Emily leads a fast-paced professional life, juggling multiple projects and deadlines. Her myopia has been a constant challenge, especially when it comes to her work tasks that require visual precision. Despite wearing glasses or contact lenses, she often experiences eye strain, headaches, and blurred vision by the end of the day. Emily realizes that she needs a solution to alleviate these issues and enhance her overall productivity.

She comes across an app specifically designed to assist individuals with visual impairments. This app offers features such as text resizing, a magnifier tool, and picture editing capabilities. Intrigued by its potential benefits, Emily decides to try it out. With the app, she can now easily adjust the text size on her smartphone or computer, allowing her to read and edit documents comfortably without straining her eyes. The magnifier tool helps her zoom in on small text or images, enabling her to comprehend and appreciate details that would otherwise be difficult to see.

The app's picture editing features also prove to be a valuable asset for Emily. She can now make quick edits and enhancements to images, giving her greater creative control and eliminating the need to rely on others for assistance. This newfound independence allows her to complete her marketing tasks efficiently and within her own timeline.

Scenarios
  • Scenario 1 - Document Editing: Emily is reviewing a client's marketing proposal on her computer. Using the app, she increases the text size, which significantly reduces eye strain and makes it easier for her to catch any errors or inconsistencies in the document.
  • Scenario 2 - Image Enhancement: Emily is designing a promotional banner for an upcoming marketing campaign. With the app's picture editing tools, she can resize images, adjust brightness and contrast, and apply filters to create visually appealing graphics. This empowers Emily to maintain creative control over her projects and produce high-quality marketing materials without relying on others for assistance.
Other Details
  • Emily enjoys exploring new technologies and is open to incorporating assistive tools into her daily routine to improve her visual experience and work efficiency.
  • She values her independence and is motivated to find solutions that allow her to overcome the challenges posed by her myopia.
  • Emily actively seeks recommendations and advice from optometrists and fellow professionals who have faced similar visual challenges in their work.

By using the app designed to assist individuals with visual impairments, Emily can overcome the limitations imposed by her myopia. The app's text resizing, magnifier, and picture editing features address her specific needs and frustrations, enhancing her productivity and overall visual experience. Emily's ability to comfortably read and edit documents, magnify small text or images, and independently edit and enhance visuals contributes to her success in her marketing career while reducing eye strain and improving her overall well-being.

Type: Primary (Persona with color blindness)

Name: Alex Martinez

Background
  • Date of Birth: 02/15/1985
  • Gender: Male
  • Location: Seattle, Washington, USA
  • Work place: Software Development Company, Engineer
  • School: Graduated with a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science from a local university (if required)
  • Technology Level: Proficient in using smartphones, computers, and various software applications
Main Points
  • Alex is a software developer who has been diagnosed with color blindness, specifically red-green color blindness.
  • He primarily works on designing user interfaces and coding front-end applications.
  • Alex faces challenges in perceiving certain color combinations accurately, which affects his ability to create visually appealing and accessible designs.
Goals
  • Develop visually inclusive user interfaces that consider the needs of color-blind users.
  • Enhance his own design skills and expand his knowledge on designing accessible interfaces.
  • Improve his efficiency and productivity in designing and coding by utilizing tools specifically designed for individuals with visual impairments.
Frustrations and Pain Points
  • Struggling to differentiate between certain colors, particularly shades of red and green, leading to potential design flaws.
  • Depending on the assistance of colleagues to verify color combinations, which slows down the design process and reduces his independence.
  • Feeling limited in his ability to fully express his creativity and contribute effectively to projects.
Detailed Description

Alex is a talented software developer in his late thirties. He has been working in the field for over a decade and specializes in designing user interfaces and coding front-end applications. However, he faces challenges due to his red-green color blindness. This visual impairment affects his ability to accurately perceive certain color combinations, which is crucial when creating visually appealing and accessible designs.

Alex often encounters difficulties distinguishing between shades of red and green, which can lead to unintended design flaws. He finds it frustrating to rely on the assistance of his colleagues to verify color choices, as it slows down his design process and reduces his independence. Additionally, he feels that his creativity is limited, as he has to constantly consider the impact of his color perception on the overall design.

Alex is motivated to develop visually inclusive user interfaces that consider the needs of color-blind users. He understands the importance of accessibility in design and wishes to contribute effectively to projects. However, he requires tools that can help him overcome his color blindness and enhance his design skills.

References
  • Color Blindness: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Color_blindness;
  • Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG): https://www.w3.org/WAI/standards-guidelines/wcag/;
Scenarios

1. Alex is working on a new project where he needs to use a color palette that includes shades of red and green. He utilizes a color blindness simulation feature in the app to identify the color combinations that are difficult for him to distinguish. With this information, he adjusts the color palette accordingly to ensure a more accessible design.

2. Alex is collaborating with a team on a web application. He uses the text resizing feature of the app to enlarge the font size, making it easier for him to read and review the code. This feature enhances his productivity and reduces the strain on his eyes.

3. Alex receives feedback on a design from a colleague who points out a potential issue with the color contrast. He uses the picture editing feature in the app to adjust the color values, improving the contrast and ensuring better visibility for color-blind users.

Other Details

1. Alex keeps up with the latest trends and advancements in web design and accessibility.

2. He actively participates in online forums and communities dedicated to color blindness and accessible design.

3. Alex is open to trying new technologies and tools that can assist him in overcoming his color blindness and improving his design skills.

Type: Primary (Persona with pink-eye-conjunctivitis)

Name: Sarah Garcia

Background
  • Date of Birth: 02/15/1990
  • Gender: Female
  • Location: Chicago, Illinois, USA
  • Work place: Organization Name, Human Resources
  • School: Graduated from University of Illinois with a degree in Communications
  • Technology Level: Intermediate; comfortable using smartphones and basic apps, but not well-versed in accessibility features
Main Points
  • Diagnosed with pink eye (conjunctivitis) a week ago
  • Experiencing redness, itching, and blurry vision in both eyes
  • Temporary visual impairment affecting daily activities
  • Seeking assistance from an app to improve visual accessibility
Goals
  • Alleviate symptoms and discomfort caused by conjunctivitis
  • Enhance visual capabilities during the recovery period
  • Ensure efficient use of technology despite temporary visual impairment
Frustrations and Pain Points
  • Limited ability to read text on screens or printed materials
  • Difficulty in recognizing faces or identifying objects from a distance
  • Dependence on others for assistance with tasks requiring visual acuity
  • Feeling of isolation due to reduced participation in regular activities
Detailed Description

Sarah Thompson is a 33-year-old marketing professional residing in Chicago. She was diagnosed with pink eye (conjunctivitis) a week ago, which has resulted in redness, itching, and blurry vision in both eyes. As a visually impaired individual during this recovery period, Sarah is seeking an app that can assist her in coping with her temporary condition.

Sarah's current visual impairment limits her ability to read text, whether on screens or printed materials. She struggles with tasks such as reading emails, articles, or even text messages. Identifying objects or recognizing faces from a distance has become challenging for her as well. These limitations have caused frustration and have made Sarah reliant on others for assistance, hindering her independence.

To overcome these challenges, Sarah is looking for an app that provides accessibility features specifically designed for individuals with visual impairments. She needs functionality that allows her to resize text, magnify content, and edit pictures, as these features would significantly enhance her ability to engage with digital and printed materials. By utilizing such an app, Sarah hopes to alleviate the discomfort caused by conjunctivitis and regain her ability to perform daily tasks with greater ease and independence.

References
  • American Academy of Ophthalmology. (2018). Conjunctivitis. Retrieved from https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/pink-eye-conjunctivitis
Scenarios
  • Scenario 1: Text resizing - Sarah receives an important email from her supervisor but struggles to read it due to her visual impairment. She opens the accessibility app on her smartphone and uses the text resizing feature to increase the font size, making the email content more legible. By utilizing this functionality, Sarah can read the email without relying on someone else to read it aloud, enhancing her independence and productivity.
  • Scenario 2: Magnifier - Sarah needs to fill out a physical form for an upcoming doctor's appointment, but her blurred vision makes it challenging to read the small print. She opens the app on her smartphone and activates the magnifier feature. By hovering her phone's camera over the form, the app magnifies the text, enabling Sarah to read and fill out the form accurately.
  • Scenario 3: Picture editing - Sarah receives a document with important graphics that she needs to review for an upcoming client presentation. Due to her visual impairment, she struggles to discern the details and make necessary edits. Using the picture editing feature in the accessibility app, Sarah can enhance the contrast, adjust brightness, or magnify specific sections of the document's graphics, enabling her to make precise edits and ensure the final version is accessible to all.
Other Details
  • Sarah's pink eye (conjunctivitis) is expected to resolve within two to three weeks.
  • She is eager to regain her visual capabilities and resume her regular activities as soon as possible.
  • Sarah is committed to exploring and utilizing technology to assist her during this temporary period of visual impairment.
